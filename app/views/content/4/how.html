First, we’ll try changing the sprite. There are four ways to do this: you can choose one from the selection in Scratch, download another image from the internet, draw one in the Scratch editor, or take a picture on your webcam (if you have one). For now, let’s just choose a sprite from Scratch’s own collection. Simply click on the little face next to where it says “New Sprite:” and the Sprite Library will pop up, and you can choose which ever sprite you want! Personally, I don’t like cats much (I prefer pets that can fly and don’t eat all my canned tuna), so I’ll choose a bat.

Careful, your commands only apply to one sprite, so when you delete your original sprite, you will delete all your code! To transfer your commands to another sprite, click on your first sprite, go to your commands, right click, and click on “duplicate”. Then click on your second sprite, and your commands will be transferred! Now you can delete your first sprite.
